Your butlers are extraordinary. A Titanium Seven Seas Society member steps aboard and feels known. Not because of a CRM. Because a person remembered: what they drink, where they like to sit, the artist their wife loves, the way they read quietly until Day 5 and seek conversation after.

But that knowledge lives in one person’s head. When that butler rotates to another ship, changes contracts, or leaves the industry, twelve voyages of intimacy reset to zero. The guest doesn’t complain. They simply don’t rebook the Regent Suite.

Ritz-Carlton solved a version of this on land with Mystique. Four Seasons is moving to app-based guest intelligence. At sea, no cruise line has solved it.

What Shorelight Does

01
It keeps what butlers already know. Preferences, observations, small moments, made permanent. The record survives crew rotation, ship changes, and fleet expansion.
02
Voice-first, built for crew. “What wine does Mrs. Henderson prefer?” Answered in two seconds, in the butler’s own language. No screens. No typing. No training manual.
03
It prompts the rare gesture. Small, precise, inexpensive, and exact to the guest. A $30 ceramics piece that becomes a story told for years. A dinner seating that reunites friends from a previous voyage.
04
It measures what matters. Not satisfaction alone, but which emotions drive rebooking, referral, and lifetime value. The feeling, connected to the financial outcome.

It Starts With Zero Integration

There is no IT project to begin. Shorelight earns its way deeper: from public signal, to your own channels, to the moments that compound into loyalty.

Start
Start with what guests already make public. No integration, no security review, no IT queue. Shorelight works from the signal each guest has already put into the world. You know who is aboard, and what would move them, before they step on deck.
Connect
Connect your channels when you are ready. Fold in the call center, email, and guest surveys on your own timetable. Each is an addition, never an overhaul. The record completes itself, first inquiry to post-cruise note, in a single view.
Shape
Shape the moments that matter. Every layer serves one question: which gesture, for which guest, at which moment. Get that right, sailing after sailing, and you build guest equity — the compounding loyalty that satisfaction scores and lifetime value only measure once it is already won or lost.

Why This Was Built for Regent

  • All-inclusive by design. Zero marginal cost of personalization. Every surprise is already “included.”
  • 750 guests per ship. Small enough for true intimacy, large enough to need a system.
  • Seven Seas Society. Repeat guests create longitudinal depth. Each voyage compounds what you know.
  • Butler culture. The instinct already exists. Shorelight lets it outlast the individual.
  • Prestige (2026). A clean start, free of legacy systems. Build it right from Day 1.
