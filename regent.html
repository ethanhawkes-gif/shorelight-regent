🧮 Methodology — Why This Read Is Defensible
For the analyst in the room

Anyone can scrape guest data — Salesforce plus AI can assemble it. The wedge isn't the data; it's the perspective: of everything you could do for a guest, which few moments carry the highest expected value against that guest's equity. Here's how every line on this platform is graded.

①
Sourced or labeled: Each fact is tagged observed (logged aboard), sourced (public/verified, with citation), or inferred (with confidence). Nothing presents as fact that isn't one.
②
Ranked by expected value: Recommendations are scored against the four equity levers — Spend, Repeat, Advocacy, Influence — and only the top few surface. The system shows the count it considered, so the ranking is auditable.
③
No fabricated dollars: Where a real figure exists it's cited; where it doesn't, the lever is stated qualitatively (high / outsized / at-risk). The platform will never hand you a number it can't defend.
④
Built for a low-risk pilot: One ship, fixed window, measurable before/after on rebooking and recognition — the kind of contained, measurable test a new-in-role president can stand behind.

💠 The Equity Flywheel
Framework

Shorelight measures guests by what compounds over a lifetime — not by invented forecasts. The doctrine: Spend → Repeat → Advocacy → Influence.

Spend: share of wallet that stays aboard (all-inclusive removes friction)

Repeat: rebooking — the perishable lever; a guest who walks off silent rarely comes back

Advocacy: what they say unpaid — a Forbes feature, a Facebook embarkation post

Influence: who books because of them — the social anchors others follow

💡
A personalized moment costs almost nothing — Regent's all-inclusive infrastructure is already in place. The risk runs the other way: a missed moment is a perishable loss. The guest leaves quietly, tells no one, and resurfaces with a competitor who wows her. Slowly, the flywheel turns against you. Shorelight keeps it turning your way — and ranks which moment, for which guest, turns it hardest.
